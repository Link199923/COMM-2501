Cultural influence and economical growth

Cultural influence:
Filmmakers from the National Film Board of Canada, like Michel Brault and Pierre Perrault, brought a unique approach to filmmaking that explore the lives of Quebec citizens portraying authentic moments and breaking away from traditional narrative structures (Delisle, Martin n.d.). This leaves a profound impact on Quebec's cinematic culture.

Economic growth:
The artistic and cultural impact of Direct Cinema suggests that the legacy of filmmakers such as Brault, Fournier, Godbout, Jutra, and Perrault has contributed to economic growth (Delisle, Martin n.d.) by continuing to entertain viewers, offer study materials, and inspire a new generation of filmmakers in Quebec (Delisle, Martin n.d.).

Quebec film international reputation

The source notes that the collaborative relationship between Michel Brault and Jean Rouch, leading to their film "Chronique d'un Èté" (Delisle, Martin n.d.) showing how Quebec's innovative filmmaking techniques gained international recognition and were showcased at film festivals and documentary seminars, highlighting the global reach and reputation of Quebec cinema's unique contributions.

The film "Pour la Suite du Monde" co-directed by Pierre Perrault and Michel Brault, not only became a key work of the documentary genre but also earned the NFB's French speaking team worldwide fame (Delisle, Martin n.d.), underscoring how specific films from Quebec's cinema movement accumulated international praise and recognition.
